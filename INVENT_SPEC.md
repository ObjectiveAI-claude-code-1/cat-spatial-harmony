Evaluate spatial harmony between the cat and its environment in the photograph. Examine the relationship between the cat and the space around it — the negative space, background elements, and foreground objects — and determine whether that relationship serves the subject. The cat should be given room to breathe without being dwarfed or diminished by its surroundings. Negative space should feel purposeful — existing to frame, cushion, or stage the cat rather than to overwhelm or abandon it. Background elements should feel like they belong to the same visual story as the cat rather than competing with it for the viewer's attention. Poor spatial harmony manifests as backgrounds that overwhelm the subject with busy textures or dominant shapes, framing so tight the cat feels claustrophobic, or emptiness so vast the cat feels abandoned. Strong spatial harmony means the environment acts as a stage for the cat — space that serves the subject rather than working against it.
